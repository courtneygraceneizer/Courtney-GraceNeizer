Hello! I’m Courtney-Grace, a fourth-year undergraduate student at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte with a concentration in bioinformatics.

I am passionate about empowering others through technology and education. I run The STEMinist Blog, a platform for young girls and women in STEM, promoting cross-cultural exchanges and empowering young girls in STEM. With contributions from female-identifying Ph.D., medical, and undergraduate students in STEM, The STEMinist Blog offers science posts, career advice, and vlogs to support the next generation of women in science and technology.

I currently work in the Luo Lab (Bioinformatics) under Dr. Elaine Luo, in collaboration with Dr. Wenyu Gao (Statistics) to apply machine learning and statistical methods to microbial data analysis.

My research focuses on building predictive models to identify protein domain features linked to high Excess Atom Fraction (EAF) values, enhancing our understanding of microbial functions in carbon cycling. You can read more about my research here.
